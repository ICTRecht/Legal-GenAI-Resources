## Red flags

**Article 8.1 — Liability limitation**
- **Issue:** Supplier's liability is capped at the fees paid in the last calendar month. For annual contracts, this typically amounts to 1/12 of the annual fee. In the event of a significant service failure or data breach, this cap may be disproportionately low relative to the customer's actual loss.
- **Recommendation:** Negotiate a higher cap (e.g. annual fees paid) or carve out gross negligence, wilful misconduct, and data protection breaches from the liability limitation.

**Article 14 — Governing law: absent**
- **Issue:** The contract contains no governing law clause. Under Dutch private international law (Rome I Regulation), the governing law would be determined by the habitual residence of the service provider — but this creates uncertainty.
- **Recommendation:** Add a governing law clause specifying Dutch law and the competent court (e.g. District Court of Amsterdam).
